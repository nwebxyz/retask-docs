This guide takes you from a brand-new account to a working project with people
and an AI Agent on it. No coding required — everything here happens in the app.

If any of the words below are unfamiliar (workspace, project, task, agent), the
[Concepts](/guides/concepts/) page explains them all in plain language. It's a
useful five-minute read before you start.

<Aside type="tip" title="New to Retask?">
  The Free plan has no time limit and needs no credit card. You get room for a
  small team, a few projects, and a handful of AI Agents to try things out.
</Aside>

## 1. Create your workspace

A **workspace** is your whole account — it holds your people, projects, and
settings.

<Steps>

1. Go to [app.retask.work](https://app.retask.work) and sign in.

2. Create a workspace and give it a name (your company or team name works well).

3. You'll land in the workspace, with the name shown in the switcher at the top.

</Steps>

![The Retask workspace with the All tasks view open.](/screenshots/tasks-board.png)

<Aside type="note">
  You can always change the workspace name, color, and description later under
  **Settings → General**. See the [Workspaces guide](/guides/workspaces/).
</Aside>

## 2. Create your first project

A **project** groups related work — usually one per app, product, or area.

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="In the app">
    Open **Projects** in the sidebar, click **New project**, give it a name
    (for example, "Website"), and you'll land on an empty **Kanban board** ready
    for tasks.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="With the CLI">
    ```bash
    # Authenticate once, then create a project
    retask auth login
    retask project create --name "Website"
    ```
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

![A project's Kanban board with Todo, In Progress, In Preview, and Done columns.](/screenshots/project-kanban.png)

## 3. Invite your team

Bring in the people you work with. Each person gets a **role** that controls what
they can do — Owner, Admin, Editor, or Viewer.

<Steps>

1. Go to **Settings → Members**.

2. Click **Invite member** and enter their email address.

3. Choose a role. **Editor** is the right choice for most teammates — they can
   create and change tasks. Use **Viewer** for people who only need to follow along.

</Steps>

![The workspace members list, showing people and their roles.](/screenshots/settings-members.png)

<Aside type="note">
  Not sure which role to pick? The [Workspaces guide](/guides/workspaces/#roles-and-permissions)
  has a table comparing what each role can do.
</Aside>

## 4. Add an AI Agent

An **Agent** is an AI worker you can assign tasks to, just like a teammate. You
configure it once by filling in a short form — there's no separate template to
wire up.

<Steps>

1. Go to **Settings → Members → Agents** and click **New agent**.

2. Fill in the basics: a **name**, an optional **description**, and a **role** —
   *Task processor* (acts on a task when its status changes) or *Task planner*
   (plans and assigns tasks automatically).

3. Choose when it runs and where: a **trigger** (for example, "when a task is
   assigned to this agent") and an **execution target** (such as "run on any
   available sandbox"). Add any **integrations** it needs, and an optional
   **agent prompt**.

4. Save, and the agent appears alongside your human members, ready to be
   assigned work.

</Steps>

<Aside type="note">
  The **Advanced** tab lets you fine-tune the environment — a sandbox startup
  command, a shutdown policy, and the session's system prompt — but the defaults
  are a fine place to start. See the [Agents guide](/guides/agents/) for the full
  set of options.
</Aside>

<Aside type="caution">
  Agents do real work on your code inside a sandbox. Start them on a low-stakes
  task first so you can watch what they do before trusting them with anything
  important.
</Aside>

## 5. Create a task and assign it

Now put it all together: add a task and hand it to a person or your new agent.

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="In the app">
    Click **+ Task** at the top, fill in a title and description, set a priority,
    and choose an assignee — a teammate or your AI Agent. Save, and it appears on
    the board.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="With the CLI">
    ```bash
    retask task create \
      --project-id <id> \
      --title "Fix login bug" \
      --priority HIGH
    ```
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

When you assign a task to an AI Agent, it runs inside an isolated
**sandbox** and opens a **session** you can watch live. The full flow is covered
in [Run an AI Agent on a task](/guides/run-agent-on-task/).
